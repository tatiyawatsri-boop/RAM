FRONTEND TO FOUNDER HACKATHON 2025

Team Terratech — students from the Digital Business Innovation program, Faculty of Information Technology, Sripatum University Khon Kaen Campus proudly won 2nd Place in the "FRONTEND TO FOUNDER HACKATHON 2025", held under the theme “Think – Code – Create: From Project to Startup.” The competition, organized by Overflow Co., Ltd., challenged participants to build an application within just 6 intense hours, capable of running seamlessly across notebooks, smartphones, and tablets. The Terratech team impressed the judges and walked away with a cash prize of 10,000 THB, along with a trophy and certificates of achievement. Their innovative project featured: One-stop Web App: A comprehensive travel planner that lets users discover destinations and map routes all in one place. Weather Update: Real-time weather tracking for confident trip planning. AI Trip Planner: Simply choose a category and budget,
and let AI design your perfect travel route automatically.

Role fo Team

In this competition, I contributed by proposing creative ideas for key features that could make our project stand out and increase our chances of reaching the final round. I also took responsibility for developing the Front-end of the web application, following the Figma design created by my teammates, and integrating it for use with the Back-end system. During the final round, I continued to share ideas on new and engaging features that would enhance our app, while also handling the Front-end development to ensure the design and functionality matched the team’s vision and user experience goals.

Students from Sripatum University, Khon Kaen Campus have successfully advanced to the Final Pitching Round as one of the Top 20 teams out of 83 nationwide in the GOSOFT HACKATHON 2025: ServiceTech Revolution competition. Thai chess

I started playing Thai chess when I was in the upper of primary school. At first, I wasn’t good at it at all I could barely move the pieces correctly. But when I entered middle school, I got the chance to play again, and this time it was serious because I had to compete on behalf of my school. In the beginning, I didn’t enjoy it much since I didn’t really understand how to play. However, as I began to build a stronger foundation and practiced more seriously, I started to like it. Thai chess, to me, is a sport where you compete more against yourself than your opponent.The more mistakes you make, the greater your chance of losing so staying focused is everything. That’s what I love about it. It trains me to think systematically and to stay calm under pressure. If you let your emotions be swayed by your opponent, you end up playing their game instead of your own. That balance between strategy and self-control is what makes Thai chess so fascinating to me.
